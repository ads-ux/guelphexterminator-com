Mouse Exterminator in Guelph: How Mice Get In & How to Stop Them

A practical guide for Guelph homeowners dealing with mice.

Quick answer: Mice enter Guelph homes through gaps as small as a dime around pipes, foundations, garage doors, and soffits — especially in September and October. Snap traps alone don't solve the problem. A professional mouse exterminator seals entry points and uses bait stations to eliminate the colony, not just individual mice.

Why Guelph Homes Are Vulnerable to Mice

Guelph's mix of older housing stock, proximity to agricultural land in Puslinch and Erin, and the large student population near the University of Guelph creates consistent mouse pressure throughout the city. Older homes in neighbourhoods like the Ward, Exhibition Park, and Downtown Guelph often have original sill plates and foundations with gaps that have never been properly sealed. Newer subdivisions in south Guelph are not immune — construction gaps around utility penetrations are frequently left unsealed by builders.

Every fall, as temperatures in Wellington County drop, field mice and house mice move toward heated structures. Guelph homes along the Stone Road, Edinburgh Road, and York Road corridors — where residential neighbourhoods border agricultural or green space — see the highest mouse pressure.

How Mice Get Into Guelph Homes

A house mouse can compress its body to fit through a gap roughly 6–7mm in diameter — about the size of a dime. Common entry points in Guelph homes include:

  • Pipe and cable penetrations — where gas lines, water pipes, electrical conduit, and dryer vents pass through exterior walls. Builder foam degrades and shrinks over time.
  • Garage doors — worn bottom seals, gaps at corners, and the gap between the door and the threshold are frequent entry points.
  • Sill plate and foundation gaps — where the wood frame of the home meets the foundation, especially in homes built before 1980.
  • Soffits and fascia — rotting or damaged soffit material gives mice roof access and entry into attic spaces.
  • Weep holes in brick — the intentional ventilation gaps in brick veneer are perfectly mouse-sized and often unprotected.
  • Drain and sewer lines — less common, but rats and larger mice can enter through floor drains and toilet drain lines.

Signs You Have Mice in Your Guelph Home

The most reliable early indicators of a mouse infestation are:

  • Droppings — small, rod-shaped, 3–6mm long. Found along walls, in the back of kitchen cabinets, behind the stove, and near food storage areas.
  • Gnaw marks — on food packaging (especially cardboard and plastic), wooden structural elements, and wiring insulation. Mice must gnaw constantly to keep their incisors trimmed.
  • Sounds at night — scratching, scurrying, or squeaking from inside walls or ceilings, most noticeable between midnight and 4am when the home is quiet.
  • Grease rub marks — dark smear marks along baseboards and wall edges where mice repeatedly travel the same path.
  • Nesting material — shredded insulation, paper, fabric, or plant material stuffed into hidden corners, behind appliances, or inside walls.

Finding a single dropping does not necessarily mean a full infestation — it may indicate a mouse that entered briefly and hasn't yet established. But any sign warrants investigation and prompt action.

The Seasonal Mouse Calendar in Guelph

Mouse activity in Guelph follows a predictable seasonal pattern:

  • September–October — peak entry season as outdoor temperatures drop. This is when most Guelph homeowners first notice signs.
  • November–February — established indoor colonies reproduce. A pair of mice produces 5–10 litters per year with 5–6 pups each. An October entry becomes a large infestation by January if untreated.
  • March–April — mouse activity may appear to slow as some mice move back outdoors, but the colony remains. Any perceived reduction is usually temporary.
  • May–August — lower activity, but mice never fully leave. Summer infestations often go undetected longer because homeowners expect the problem to be seasonal.

Why DIY Mouse Control Fails

Snap traps and glue boards catch individual mice but don't address why mice are in the home. Without sealing entry points, new mice replace those caught within days — the colony simply replenishes itself from outside.

Over-the-counter bait products (rodenticides) can reduce the population but create secondary problems: mice often die inside walls, causing odour issues for weeks. And without exclusion, the colony is never fully eliminated.

After spending weeks on DIY products, most Guelph homeowners end up calling a professional anyway — by which point the infestation has grown and the treatment cost is higher.

What a Professional Mouse Exterminator Does in Guelph

A professional mouse control program in Guelph typically includes:

  1. Full perimeter inspection — identifying all potential and confirmed entry points on the exterior of the home
  2. Entry point sealing — using steel wool, copper mesh, caulk, and hardware cloth to permanently close gaps. This is the most important step.
  3. Bait station placement — tamper-resistant stations placed in key interior locations where mouse activity has been confirmed
  4. Return visits — checking stations and assessing whether the population is declining
  5. Prevention advice — identifying food storage, clutter, and landscaping issues that attract mice

The cost of a professional mouse exclusion program in Guelph typically runs $300–$700 depending on the size of the home and number of entry points. This includes the exclusion work and at least one follow-up visit. Most programs include a warranty period of 60–90 days.
